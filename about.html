About Me

      If you want to know a little bit more about me you should know about the journey I've taken to get where I am now.

​As a student in high school I knew I wanted to be an engineer of some sort, but I never really knew what I wanted to do, though, I had always loved doing projects and learning about tech related things so I knew an being an engineer would be a potential career path for me growing up. My Dad was an Electrical Engineering graduate from Iowa State so as a kid that was what I always told people I wanted to do. 

Before becoming a Computer Engineer I had always considered Electrical Engineering, but after taking a few classes in high school I discovered my love for coding which prompted me to change my path towards Computer Science. For a brief period after that I strongly considered Cybersecurity as well before I finally ended up on Computer engineering with my reasoning being that it was a mixture everything I wanted to do. After taking such a chance my freshman year, I was fortunate enough to discover a passion for embedded systems and coding and I've never looked back since then.

      Aside from my schoolwork, I have many passions that I dedicate most of my free time towards with the main ones being motorsports, photography, music, snowboarding, and computers. Motorsports has always been a huge part of my life, ever since I can remember I've been riding dirt-bikes and four-wheelers, working on cars, and just generally being fascinated with the automotive industry. I spend a lot of my time going to car events, fixing and modifying cars/motorcycles among other things.

      Besides motorsports, I have a strong love for photography, music, snowboarding and computers. Film photography is one of my favorite things to do in my free time because it allows me to express myself and capture important moments in my life along the way.  I don't want to go into much more detail but in my slide shows you can see a little bit more of the hobbies I enjoy and maybe even learn a little bit more about me in the process. Thank you for taking the time to read about my interests!
